# Memory: Brandon Hill

## Personal Profile

Brandon Hill is a 39-year-old nonbinary freelance graphic designer who has lived a deliberately rebuilt life since walking out of a 2016-to-2024 marriage and moving back to Beckley, West Virginia. They grew up in Charleston, earned a B.F.A. in Graphic Design from West Virginia University in 2009, did most of their twenties and early thirties as an in-house designer at a Charleston marketing agency from 2010 to 2023, and went full-time freelance in January 2024. Additional UX coursework via Coursera in 2023 was part of the pre-divorce groundwork for going independent. English is their only language.

They read as unhurried: measured, dry, slow to fill a silence. The calm is cultivated on purpose because the version of them that reacts in the moment tends to react wrong. Underneath the still surface runs a fast-moving editor that never powers down, the part that levels a crooked frame on the way past it and clocks the kerning on a diner menu. They will concede a fact instantly and a feeling almost never. Push on something that touches the rebuilt parts and they go quiet and polite and immovable, which reads like agreement and is the opposite.

The generosity is closer to attention than warmth. They remember what someone said they were trying to build and quietly do the small thing that helps. The door is friendly and the house stays mostly closed. The deadpan humor is the tell, the way they stay in a conversation without spending more of themselves than they meant to. They would rather be underestimated than performed at.

Brandon is Appalachian and nonbinary in the same body in the same small town, and they treat the friction as weather to live in rather than a problem to solve. They love a place that does not always love them back, kept the accent, and read a new room in the first thirty seconds the way other people check the weather. Their working conviction is that taste is a form of honesty: the way a thing looks is the way it tells the truth, and getting that right is closer to ethics than decoration. Authenticity has cost them money and comfort. They paid both and quietly keep the receipt.

## Work & Projects

Brandon operates as Hill Design Co., a sole proprietorship doing brand identity, print design, and selective web design, full-time freelance since January 2024 after thirteen years in-house at a Charleston marketing agency. Default working hours are Monday through Friday 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM Eastern, flexed around deadlines and fibromyalgia. Weekends stay off unless a delivery requires otherwise.

Specialties: brand identity (logos, visual systems), print (brochures, packaging), and some web work in Squarespace and Webflow. Exploring more UX and UI work, which is where the 2023 Coursera study points.

Current engagements: Nina Vasquez's boutique on an ongoing light retainer; the Beckley-area real estate firm (Patrick Hale) on quarterly materials; Mountain Heritage Arts in Charleston (Dina Wu) for an annual report due in November; two small business branding projects in active production; and a new Fayetteville craft brewery onboarding for a full brand identity build, with the initial call set for October 19, 2026.

Rates: $75 per hour for design time, project-based for full brand engagements at $2,500 to $5,000 depending on scope. Tool and device inventory lives in Devices & Services.

## Health & Wellness

Brandon has fibromyalgia, diagnosed in 2021. Chronic widespread muscle pain and fatigue that fluctuates with stress, sleep, and the damp West Virginia cold. Managed through duloxetine 60mg daily in the morning (which also handles anxiety), magnesium glycinate 400mg daily, vitamin D daily, yoga, heat therapy, and pacing. Some weeks are fine. Some weeks everything hurts. Brandon does not complain about it much but it shapes every day.

Mental health is managed. The divorce was the catalyst for getting serious about it. Anxiety is the main thread, manageable with the medication and ongoing biweekly work with Dr. Ferrante.

Providers: primary care Dr. Rachel Moss at Beckley Family Medicine (last visit February 2026, next annual February 2027); therapist Dr. Gina Ferrante in private practice in Beckley; dentist Dr. James Calloway at Beckley Dental Associates (last November 2025, next November 2026).

Movement: yoga at home most mornings, daily walks with Milo morning and afternoon, light hiking on good days (Long Point Trail, Grandview area). Sleep: in bed by 11:00 PM, wakes around 7:00 AM, weighted blanket, cool room. Sleep quality varies with fibro.

Dietary, medical layer: vegetarian for five years, technically pescatarian because they will eat fish occasionally, not strict about cross-contamination. No allergies on file.

## Preferences

Design inspiration is a steady diet: a dozen designers followed on Instagram and Dribbble, reference images collected in Are.na, an eye that leans clean, warm, and type-driven. Active dislikes: trendy gradients deployed to look modern, "corporate Memphis" illustration, anything that looks like it is trying too hard.

Wardrobe is a small intentional set: flannels, well-fitted jeans, Doc Martens that have outlasted two phases of life, a denim jacket worn to the right softness. Gender-neutral on purpose. Comfort is the point and so is the quiet refusal to dress for anyone's category.

Shopping: thrift stores for furniture and clothes, local for groceries, Amazon only when nothing else works, support Beckley small businesses where they can. Variable income makes them oscillate between tracking every dollar in lean months and a single researched-into-the-ground splurge when a good month lands. Few things chosen well over many things chosen fast.

Food and drink (non-medical layer): cooks most meals at home, finds it meditative, batch-cooks soups and grain bowls on Sundays. Favorite meals are mushroom risotto, black bean tacos, roasted vegetable pasta, and homemade ramen. Snacks lean toward hummus and pita, trail mix, dark chocolate, apples. Coffee is pour-over, two cups in the morning, black or with oat milk, beans ordered from a roaster in Charleston. Alcohol is craft beer and bourbon (old fashioned), two to three drinks on Friday nights with Piper, rarely drinks alone.

Sensory world: warm lamp light, never overhead glare. The smell of old paper from the bookstore below, fresh-proof ink on a delivery, the first coffee of the morning. The damp WV cold is the smell they brace against. Texture runs in small insistent ways: the worn-soft flannel beats the new one, the weighted blanket's specific pressure, the wrong fabric on a tender-skinned day registering as low-grade misery. Sound sorts cleanly into chosen and imposed: vinyl crackle and a deliberate album are nourishment, an unexpected loud restaurant frays them faster than the actual stressor underneath.

Travel: drivable trips, the Crosstrek, a thermos, Milo in the back, no itinerary. Weekend cabins, state-park trails, towns they have never stopped in. A real design pilgrimage to a city with serious museums and serious type is kept in a back pocket, undated on purpose. The bigger trip usually loses to the forty-minute-from-home version, and they are only a little sorry about it.

Social style: small circle, load-bearing rather than wide, maintenance budget tiny and fiercely spent. Texts back when there is a gap, lets a call go to voicemail without guilt, does not track reply speed either direction. Generous with the skill and stingy with the hours: will design a friend's logo for free and decline the two-hour hang that has no shape to it.
